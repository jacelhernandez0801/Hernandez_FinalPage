Childhood Years

               On August 1, 2003, in the town of Mamburao, Occidental Mindoro, a girl named Jacel Christelle C. Hernandez was born, and that girl happens to be me1! I have two siblings, Jade and Jon, and my parents are Menchie and Jonathan2. As I start telling my story, let me first describe my younger self as a jolly, kikay and playful kid, or as they say, an extroverted one3. My favorite color is pink4. I love to eat sinigang, and my favorite thing to do is to travel5. As a matter of fact, I've been to Hong Kong Disneyland with my family in my childhood years, and it is the most unforgettable experience a child can have6. Maybe most of what I remember started from when I was 4 years old, around the year 20077. That's when I began attending school at Early Skills Learning Center and I spent 3 years of my life there, from nursery, kindergarten, to preparatory8. It's where I experienced many firsts, from learning different things to making friends9. I even remember crying because I didn't want to be left by mom in the classroom10. I also recall our school's foundation events, where we would parade in our town wearing different costumes11. I had the chance to be Tinkerbell, Lady Gaga, and many more with my costumes12. My parents would really make an effort in creating our float for the parade in our town13.

              In my elementary years at Mamburao Central School, it was much bigger than my previous school, so I needed time to adjust14. Life became more challenging with many new faces to meet and being in a public school meant we had to help in cleaning15. However, this experience made me responsible16. Although, truth be told, my motivation was different at school because I was more afraid of the teachers than at home, where I'd play as soon as I arrived17. Graduating from grade 6 in my school was a bit emotional for me. I had to bid farewell to the teachers who molded me into who I am and to my classmates with whom I shared a strong bond with18.

              My childhood vacations were truly special, all thanks to my family for letting us experience it19. They always made sure to bring us cousins together, even if we came from different towns, just to make our family complete20. That's why I grew up close to my cousins, especially on my mother's side. Most of my cherished childhood memories are filled with joy during vacations in San Jose, Occidental Mindoro, which happens to be my mom's hometown21. They went out of their way to bring our family together, especially during holidays like Christmas22. Growing up was a blast as I got to play, go on vacations, have epic sleepovers, wrestle, and just have a blast with my cousins23. It was a whole different level of fun24! Life was easy in my childhood25. It was simple because I was just an innocent kid, thinking about playing, sleeping, eating, and sometimes studying26. And, of course, there were those times when I'd get into little arguments with my siblings and cousins, giving our parents a headache with our mischief27.

Teenage Years

            In my teenage years, I enrolled at Occidental Mindoro National High School, where I took an exam to get into the first section known as the special science class1. High school life is said to be tough, but it turned out to be the most memorable for me2. Engaging in various activities, tests, and unexpected extracurriculars helped me grow3.

              Facing reality and various challenges during high school shaped me4. Before diving into that, let me share a story of when I joined the Girl Scouts, and had an opportunity to participate in an event in United States of America, a once-in-a-lifetime experience5. Grateful for the financial support extended by my aunt, I was able to represent our country during my eighth-grade year6. It was a proud moment, facing challenges and becoming independent7.Despite being young, I conquered all the challenges, and after a week of camping, my aunt fetched me, and we explored what the United States of America can offer8.Moving on to grade 10, a date etched in my memory is March 15, 2020 as I should be graduating junior high school,but it got suspended due to the COVID-19 pandemic10. The rest, as we know, is history11. I also decided to continue my senior high school in Occidental Mindoro National High School due to practical reasons, considering the challenges of having online classes in our province, often dubbed as the brownout capital of the Philippines12.

              Senior high school was not easy due to the self-learning nature of modular classes, especially with restrictions on going out13.My nightmare extended to October 2020 when I was in Grade 11 I was diagnosed with Lupus, an autoimmune disease in which my own immune system is attacking its own tissues14. My lupus was detected during the midst of COVID-19 pandemic, so it was very challenging for me and my family to withstand this health problem15. Fortunately, unlike most cases, my organs are not affected, and it was detected immediately, but still, this challenge has made me the strong woman that I am today due to the pressure, medical conditions, expenses, and social changes that impacted my own view of myself16. Even if it is hard for me most of the time, I motivated myself that with the help of my family and with God nothing is impossible in dealing with the most difficult problems even this health implication that I have17. I now mark four years in my journey as a lupus warrior with the help of medications and regular checkup18. Amidst this journey, I had the privilege of celebrating my 18th birthday with a small gathering with the closest people to my heart19.My teenage years were unforgettable, showing me my strength in conquering challenges20. Embracing the belief that everything happens for a reason, I am hopeful that the experiences I've encountered will bring lasting blessings that stay with me forever21.

College Years

              Adulthood truly set in for a provincial girl venturing into the city1. I enrolled at the University of the East Manila, taking up Bachelor of Science in Information Technology2. I chose this course based on my realizations during the pandemic3. Initially, I leaned towards a science field course, but circumstances led me here due to the high demand for IT and the possibility of working from home because as you know I should also think of whatâ€™s best for my condition4.

              College life presented challenges as we embraced independence, handling household chores and making significant decisions such as studying in Manila, far from my hometown5. Numerous adjustments were necessary because I had no choice because this is what I want so I needed to be physically, mentally, and emotionally prepared to conquer this challenge6.But I am grateful for the opportunity to study in this prestigious university7. Not everyone gets the chance, so no matter how tough it gets, I won't waste this opportunity8.

              During my freshman year, the first semester was still conducted online, and I stayed in Manila due to frequent brownouts in our province9. While online learning presented challenges, particularly with more schoolwork, the added difficulty was coping with homesickness10. But thankfully, I overcame it and managed to finish the semester with new learnings11. Freshman year, second semester I had a different experience with face-to-face classes12. It was exciting and a bit overwhelming as I met new people and interacted with professors13. It was challenging initially, but studying hard helped me realize that face-to-face learning provides a more enriching experience than online classes14.

              Now that I'm in my sophomore year, every day comes with its challenges, especially as our workload becomes more demanding15. College has been a bittersweet journey, where every day is a challenge, but having the right people around makes it easier as we share the bonds of happiness and hardships together16. As they say, patience is a virtue17. Despite battling with an illness, it won't hinder my pursuit of success in life18. Instead, it becomes my motivation to excel even further in my studies19. In life, we may encounter numerous challenges, yet through resilience and unwavering faith in God, we shall survive, as everything happens for a reason20.
