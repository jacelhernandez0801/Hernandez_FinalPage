Emily in Paris

Director: Andrew Fleming

         "Emily in Paris" follows the story of Emily Cooper, a young marketing executive from Chicago, who unexpectedly lands her dream job in Paris. Thrust into the world of high fashion and romance, Emily navigates cultural differences and workplace challenges while embracing the vibrant city life. As she balances her professional aspirations with love interests and new friendships, Emily's journey unfolds with humor, drama, and plenty of Parisian charm.

What's Wrong with Secretary Kim

Director: Park Joon-hwa

        "What's Wrong with Secretary Kim" centers on Lee Young-joon, a narcissistic vice president, and his loyal secretary, Kim Mi-so. When Kim Mi-so decides to resign after nine years, Young-joon realizes his feelings for her. As their relationship develops, they confront secrets from their pasts, exploring love, forgiveness, and personal growth amidst workplace dynamics. With humor and heart, the series navigates the complexities of office romance and the journey towards self-discovery.

Young Sheldon

Director: Jon Favreau

         "Young Sheldon" offers a glimpse into the early life of Sheldon Cooper, the beloved character from "The Big Bang Theory." Set in East Texas during the late 1980s and early 1990s, the show follows Sheldon as he grapples with the challenges of adolescence, family dynamics, and his remarkable intellect. With humor and heart, the series explores Sheldon's journey of self-discovery and academic prowess, all while highlighting the quirks and idiosyncrasies that make him such a memorable character.

2 Broke Girls

Director: Michael Patrick King and Whitney Cummings

         "2 Broke Girls" follows the unlikely friendship between Max Black, a street-smart waitress from a working-class background, and Caroline Channing, a former socialite who has fallen on hard times. Together, they navigate the ups and downs of life while working at a Brooklyn diner and dreaming of opening their own cupcake business. With sharp wit and comedic banter, the show explores their misadventures, romantic pursuits, and aspirations for a better future, all while serving up laughs and heartwarming moments along the way.

The Good Bad Mother

Director: Shim Na-yeon

"The Good Bad Mother" revolves around the life of Violet, a single mother trying to balance her career as a lawyer with raising her teenage daughter, Rosie. When Rosie begins to rebel against her mother's strict rules, Violet must confront her own past mistakes while navigating the challenges of modern parenting. As Violet grapples with guilt, self-doubt, and the pressures of society's expectations, she learns valuable lessons about love, forgiveness, and the true meaning of motherhood. Through humor, drama, and heartfelt moments, the series explores the complexities of family dynamics and the journey towards self-acceptance and growth.

2 Good To Be True

Director: Mae Cruz-Alviar

"Too Good to Be True" starring KathNiel (Kathryn Bernardo and Daniel Padilla) follows the story of Sarah and David, a seemingly perfect couple deeply in love. However, their lives take a dramatic turn when a charming stranger enters their lives, offering them a chance at unimaginable wealth and success. As they navigate the allure of this mysterious offer, Sarah and David's relationship is put to the ultimate test, forcing them to confront their deepest fears and desires. With twists and turns at every corner, the series explores the complexities of love, trust, and the price of chasing dreams that may be too good to be true.
